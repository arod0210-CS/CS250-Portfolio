### Reflection

#### How do I interpret user needs and implement them into a program? How does creating user stories help with this?

I interpret user needs by looking at what the user is trying to accomplish and then turning those needs into features the program can provide. Instead of only thinking about the code, I try to understand the user’s goal, why the feature matters, and what would make the program useful. Creating user stories helps because it explains the feature from the user’s point of view. A user story makes it easier to understand who needs the feature, what they need, and why they need it. This also helps developers and testers know what should be built and how to confirm that it works correctly.

#### How do I approach developing programs? What Agile processes do I hope to incorporate into my future development work?

I approach developing programs by breaking the work into smaller parts and focusing on one feature at a time. First, I try to understand the requirements, then I plan the main features, build the program, test it, and make improvements. Agile helped me see the importance of working in smaller increments instead of waiting until the end to test or review everything. In my future development work, I hope to use Agile processes such as sprint planning, user stories, backlog organization, regular testing, daily check-ins, and retrospectives. These practices can help me stay organized, adjust to changes, and continue improving my work.

#### What does it mean to be a good team member in software development?

Being a good team member in software development means communicating clearly, being dependable, asking questions when something is unclear, and supporting the rest of the team. Software development is not only about writing code. It also requires teamwork between developers, testers, product owners, Scrum Masters, and other stakeholders. A good team member shares progress, explains blockers, listens to feedback, and helps the team stay focused on the user’s needs. In an Agile environment, teamwork is important because the team must inspect, adapt, and improve during each sprint.
